# Construction Philosophy

## Core Principle

Constructibility is more important than literal realism.

The final piece must:
- read clearly
- survive shaping and glue-up
- and remain visually elegant in wood.

---

# Simplification

Simplification is not compromise.

Reducing:
- segmentation
- microscopic detail
- fragile geometry
- and visual clutter

often improves:
- readability
- elegance
- and durability.

---

# Wood Grain As Detail

Wood itself provides:
- texture
- movement
- visual richness
- and organic complexity.

Not every detail must be explicitly segmented.

---

# How to handle adjancent pieces
- if multiple pieces are cut from a common block or even from similar wood then gently chamfer edges to highlight the boarder
- but if adjacent pieces have high contrast color like maple and walnut then this chamfer may not be needed 

---

# Subsection gluing
- If possible find adjacent pieces that form an unambigous tight fit and shape the boarder if needed and pre-glue.  This will save time and stress during final glue up
- This also applies to major subsections of a larger piece such as a head on an animal.  It may be common to have the head with many dozens of pieces but is 'attached' to the adjacent body via a few pieces.  It may be possible to build and shape and glue the head separate from the body and then join at final glue-up.

---

# AI Workflow Philosophy

AI-generated images are:
- composition inspiration
- atmosphere exploration
- pose references

Final intarsia geometry is manually interpreted and redesigned.

The important step is converting:
“image logic”
into
“construction logic”.

---

# Detail Threshold

Some details fail below a certain physical scale.

Examples:
- feather segmentation
- tiny fingers
- isolated thin lines
- excessive interior cuts

When detail becomes fragile or visually noisy:
- simplify
- imply
- or remove.

---

# Layer Philosophy

Optional Inkscape layers are valuable for experimentation.

Examples:
- optional stump
- optional arrows
- alternate path details
- alternate foreground balance

Complexity should be additive, not mandatory.

---

# Emotional Readability

The emotional clarity of the scene matters more than perfect realism.

The viewer primarily perceives:
- silhouette
- posture
- contour
- contrast
- atmosphere

rather than microscopic detail.
